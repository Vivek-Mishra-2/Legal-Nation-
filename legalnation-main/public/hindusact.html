Hindu Succession Act:-

1. A(Father) is having two children B(Son) and C(Daughter), and after some years C got married to someone and started living with her husband, and A is having the property of worth Rs. 8 Crores. And after the death of Father when C claimed at the property her brother B refused it by saying that as you have your own family that’s why you don’t have any right in the Father’s Property. So, is B’s Statement valid or not? Explain.

(Refer to Section 6 of Hindu Succession Act)

2. A is an orphan, who by his will became a successful self-made business tycoon, and he married to B who is also an orphan, and they have a child, as they were living happily but one day an accident took place and the whole family died in the accident, now explain what will happen to the Property of A.

(Refer to Section 29 of Hindu Succession Act)

3. A is having two children B(Son) and C(Daughter), as B finds out that his father is writing a will regarding the distribution of property worth Rs. 8 Crores. He somehow managed to solemnize the marriage of his sister, and after marriage she went with her husband, and he(B) and his father(A) are alone in the house, he killed his father with a motive to acquire the inherited property as soon as possible. Now in which proportion did the property will be distributed among B and C ? Explain.

(Refer to Section 6 and 25 of Hindu Succession Act)
